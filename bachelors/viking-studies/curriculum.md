# Bachelor of Science in Viking Studies — University of Yggdrasil, 2040

## ᚠ Overview: Children of the North Wind

Viking Studies is the interdisciplinary investigation of Scandinavia's most transformative era — the Viking Age (c. 793–1066 CE) — through archaeology, history, literature, linguistics, and material culture. In 2040, the field has been revolutionized by ancient DNA (aDNA) analysis, isotope provenancing, LiDAR landscape surveys, and digital reconstruction of settlement patterns.

Graduates understand the Norse world not as caricature but as a complex, interconnected civilization that shaped Europe, the North Atlantic, and beyond.

## ᚱ Core Knowledge Domains

### The Viking World System
- **Homelands**: Denmark, Norway, Sweden — distinct political ecologies
- **Western expansion**: Shetland, Orkney, Faroes, Iceland, Greenland, Vinland
- **Eastern routes**: Volga trade route to Constantinople & Baghdad, Rus' principalities
- **Southern contact**: Danelaw in England, Normandy, Mediterranean raids
- **Self-perception**: farmers, traders, raiders — not a single identity but many

### Literary Sources
- **Poetic Edda** — mythological and heroic poems (Vǫlundarkviða, Hávamál, Vǫluspá)
- **Prose Edda** — Snorri Sturluson's systematic mythology (Gylfaginning, Skáldskaparmál)
- **Sagas of Icelanders** (Íslendingasögur) — Egils saga, Njáls saga, Laxdæla saga
- **Kings' sagas** (Konungasögur) — Heimskringla, Morkinskinna
- **Runic inscriptions** — primary contemporary evidence (over 6,000 Norse runestones)
- **Continental sources** — Adam of Bremen, Rimbert, Ibn Fadlan, Anglo-Saxon Chronicle

### Archaeological Evidence
- **Ship burials**: Oseberg, Gokstad, Tune — the vessel as metaphor
- **Trading centers**: Hedeby, Birka, Kaupang, Ribe — networks of exchange
- **Fortresses**: Trelleborg, Aggersborg, Fyrkat — circular ramparts of the Jelling dynasty
- **Hoard evidence**: Silver economies, Islamic dirhams, hack-silver, arm rings
- **Settlement archaeology**: Longhouses, pit houses, field systems, boar houses
- **aDNA revolution**: Strontium isotope provenancing, genome-wide ancestry analysis

### Language & Literature
- **Old Norse** (Old West Norse → Icelandic, Faroese; Old East Norse → Danish, Swedish)
- **Runic writing**: Elder Futhark (150–700 CE), Younger Futhark (800–1100 CE), Medieval Futhork
- **Skaldic dróttkvætt**: 8-line stanza, internal rhyme (aðalhending + skothending), kenning chains
- **Eddic metres**: fornyrðislag, ljóðaháttr, málaháttr
- **Kenning system**: base-word + determinant (whale-road = sea, tree of battles = warrior)

## ᛏ 2040-Era Advances in Viking Studies

### Digital Archaeology
- **LiDAR landscape surveys** — revealing buried Viking Age settlements without excavation
- **Photogrammetric 3D reconstruction** — ship burials, rune stones digitized at sub-millimeter resolution
- **GIS spatial analysis** — settlement patterns, trade networks, territorial boundaries
- **Satellite remote sensing** — detecting buried structures via soil moisture and vegetation stress
- **AI-assisted runic reading** — CNN-based transcription of weathered inscriptions

### Biomolecular archaeology
- **Ancient DNA (aDNA)** — genome-wide analysis of Viking Age individuals revealing migration patterns
- **Strontium isotopes** — provenancing individuals to specific geological regions
- **Proteomics** — collagen analysis for species identification in food residues
- **Lipid residue analysis** — identifying vessel contents (dairy, meat, fish, beer)
- **Paleoproteomics** — reading proteins from 1000-year-old bones

### Computational Philology
- **NLP on Old Norse corpora** — automated parsing, dependency trees, semantic networks
- **Stylometric analysis** — identifying saga authorship and interpolation via computational methods
- **Digital rune databases** — full searchable corpora of all known inscriptions with 3D models
- **Machine translation** — Old Norse ↔ Modern English with scholarly disambiguation

## ᛞ Capstone Requirements

Original research thesis (10,000–15,000 words) with:
- Primary source analysis (runic, literary, or archaeological)
- Engagement with current scholarly debate
- Digital methodology component (GIS, aDNA interpretation, or computational philology)
- Oral defense before faculty committee

**Recent Capstone Topics**:
- Strontium isotope evidence for female mobility in the Danelaw
- LiDAR identification of previously unknown Viking Age settlements in Uppland
- Computational stylometry of the sagas attributed to Snorri Sturluson
- Trade network reconstruction using dirham hoard distribution mapping
