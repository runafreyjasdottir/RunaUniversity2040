**Motto:** *Af innsæi kemur heimur* — "From insight, a world emerges"

---

## Program Overview

The MS in AI World Modeling Systems Design is an advanced, research-oriented program for engineers who seek to push the boundaries of what AI world models can represent, simulate, and predict. Building on the BS foundation, the MS program focuses on advanced WYRD Protocol research, multi-world simulation, emergent narrative, hybrid symbolic-neural world modeling, and the unsolved problems at the intersection of world modeling and AI safety. Students engage with the University's Yggdrasil Research Lab and the NornLabs WYRD Consortium.

Graduates pursue roles as principal world architects, WYRD Protocol researchers, narrative AI leads, and multi-agent simulation directors.

## Year Five — Advanced World Architecture

### WM501 — Advanced WYRD Protocol: Branching Worlds and Counterfactuals
**4 Credits** | *The Weave Unraveled*

The WYRD Protocol at its most advanced. This course covers branching world lines (multiple simultaneous futures), counterfactual world state (what would have happened if...), retrospective editing with audit trails, and the Skuld Branch Prediction Engine. Students work with experimental WYRD v4.0 features and contribute to the protocol's development. The course requires a protocol extension proposal that adds a novel branching semantics to WYRD, defended before the WYRD Standards Committee (faculty panel).

### WM503 — Formal Verification of World Models
**4 Credits** | *Heimdall's Gaze Made Precise*

World models must be verified — formally, rigorously, and continuously. This course covers advanced model checking for world state: property specification in the Heimdall Temporal Logic (HTL), invariant preservation proofs across state transitions, bounded model checking for WYRD Protocol compliance, and the verification of emergent narrative properties. Students use the Yggdrasil Verifier toolchain to formally verify a complex multi-agent world simulation. Research project: find and fix a real bug in the University's WYRD v4.0 implementation using formal methods.

### WM505 — Hybrid Symbolic-Neural World Modeling
**4 Credits** | *Where Rune Meets Neuron*

The frontier of world modeling: seamlessly integrating symbolic reasoning (rules, logic, knowledge graphs) with neural pattern recognition (learned world dynamics, perception models, language-grounded simulation). This course covers the Huginn-Muninn Architecture: Huginn (thought) provides fast neural predictions, Muninn (memory) provides verified symbolic constraints, and the system reconciles them in real-time. Students implement hybrid models that combine LLM-based world dynamics with WYRD-compliant symbolic state rules, achieving both flexibility and reliability.

### WM507 — Emergent Narrative and Dramatic AI
**4 Credits** | *The Skald's Discovery*

Graduate-level narrative engine design focused on emergence rather than authorship. How do stories arise from the interactions of autonomous agents in a simulated world? This course covers: dramatic arc detection in multi-agent system traces, narrative tension modeling, character recognition algorithms, and the Saga Engine — the University's framework for detecting and shaping emergent narrative. Students build worlds that *tell stories* without a human author, then analyze what narratives emerged and why. Named for the Skalds — those who discover stories already written in the bark of Yggdrasil.

### WM509 — Advanced Multi-Agent Simulation Architecture
**4 Credits** | *The Great Þing*

Scaling world simulations to thousands of autonomous agents requires fundamentally different architectures. This course covers: spatial hashing for massive world state, level-of-detail agent simulation (agents simplify when unobserved), hierarchical social modeling, and the Althing Architecture for agent assemblies at scale. Students study computational complexity bounds for multi-agent simulation and implement a world that stably supports 10,000+ agents with individual memory systems. Performance benchmarking against the NornLabs reference implementation is required.

## Year Six — Research and Thesis

### WM601 — World Model Safety and Alignment
**4 Credits** | *The Boundaries of Midgard*

When a world model drifts from reality, its agents drift from safety. This course covers alignment in world modeling: ensuring that simulated worlds faithfully represent stakeholder values, that emergent behavior stays within acceptable bounds, and that world model errors don't propagate into harmful agent actions. Topics include: value-grounded world state constraints, simulated ethics auditing, the Midgard Boundary Protocol for detecting world model misalignment, and case studies of real-world misalignment incidents. Students design a world model alignment framework and demonstrate its effectiveness on a benchmark suite.

### WM603 — Reality-Virtual Synchronization: Advanced Bifröst
**4 Credits** | *The Rainbow Bridge Reinforced*

Advanced reality-virtual bridging: real-time sensor fusion, ontological alignment between physical and virtual state, the handling of uncertainty and contradiction in bridged worlds, and the Bifröst v2 Protocol for low-latency, high-fidelity synchronization. Students work with live data streams from the Yggdrasil Campus IoT network, building world models that incorporate real physical observations and produce predictions that are tested against future observations. Research project: achieve measurable prediction accuracy on a real-world domain using a Bifröst-bridged world model.

### WM605 — World Modeling for Scientific Discovery
**4 Credits** | *Sindri's Forge: Worlds That Discover*

The most ambitious application of world modeling: using simulated worlds to discover truths about the real one. This course covers the use of AI world models as scientific instruments: simulating drug interactions, climate dynamics, economic cascades, and social phenomena. Students learn to design world models that generate testable hypotheses, run in silico experiments, and identify novel predictions. Named for Sindri, the dwarf who forged treasures through knowledge of the world's hidden properties. Research project required: use a WYRD-compliant world model to generate a novel scientific hypothesis and validate it against real data.

### WM607 — Research Methods in World Modeling
**4 Credits** | *The Scholar's Compass*

Graduate research methods specific to world modeling: designing controlled simulation experiments, measuring emergent properties, statistical validation of world model predictions, human-subject evaluation of world realism, and the ethics of world simulation research. Students develop their thesis research plan in this course, with peer review and faculty mentoring. Includes training in the Yggdrasil Research Lab's experimental infrastructure and the WYRD Benchmark Suite.

### WM609 — Thesis: AI World Modeling Systems Design
**4 Credits** | *The Final Tapestry*

The MS thesis. Students conduct original research in AI World Modeling, producing a thesis that contributes a novel WYRD Protocol extension, world simulation architecture, verification method, or governance framework. Thesis proposals must be approved by the WM faculty committee. The thesis defense is a public event. Exceptional theses are published in the Yggdrasil Annals and the WYRD Symposium proceedings. The best thesis of each year receives the Silver Raven Award.
