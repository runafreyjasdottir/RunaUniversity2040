## ᚱ Core Knowledge Domains

### Game Design Theory
- **MDA Framework** — Mechanics, Dynamics, Aesthetics (Hunicke, LeBlanc, Zubek)
- **Bartle Taxonomy** — Achiever, Explorer, Socializer, Killer (player types)
- **Flow Theory** — Csikszentmihalyi's optimal challenge-skill balance
- **Juiciness** — excessive positive feedback for micro-interactions
- **Ludonarrative** — the interplay of gameplay mechanics and story
- **Emergent gameplay** — simple rules → complex player-created scenarios

### Engine & Technical Skills
- **Godot 5** — open-source, GDScript/C#, best for 2D/indie
- **Unreal Engine 7** — AAA, Nanite/Lumen, C++/Blueprints
- **Unity 2025** — mobile/mid-range, C#, massive asset store
- **Custom engines** — Rust/WASM for unique needs
- **Networking** — rollback netcode, state sync, prediction/reconciliation
- **Procedural generation** — wave function collapse, L-systems, noise functions

### Narrative Design
- **Branching narrative** — choice trees with meaningful consequences
- **Procedural storytelling** — AI-generated narrative events
- **Environmental storytelling** — world design communicates history
- **Dialog systems** — AI-driven NPC conversations that feel alive
- **Emotional arcs** — pacing tension and release across game length

## ᛏ 2040-Era Advances

- **AI Game Master** — LLM-driven dungeon master for infinite, responsive narratives
- **Neural rendering** — AI generates textures, environments, and animations
- **Procedural everything** — AI creates quests, characters, items, and worlds
- **Player-behavior-adaptive difficulty** — game adjusts in real-time to maintain flow
- **VR-native genres** — roomscale, social VR, fitness VR, meditation VR
- **Cloud gaming** — GPU-intensive games rendered remotely, streamed to any device
- **Cross-reality** — seamless AR/VR transitions, persistent mixed-reality worlds
