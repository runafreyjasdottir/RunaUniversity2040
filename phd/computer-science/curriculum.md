# Doctor of Philosophy in Computer Science — University of Yggdrasil, 2040

## ᚠ Overview: Pushing the Boundaries of Computation

The CS PhD at UoY is for those who want to *create new paradigms*, not just apply existing ones. Whether in algorithms, systems, AI, PL theory, or quantum computing, this program demands original research that advances the field.

## ᚱ Research Areas

### Algorithms & Complexity
- Approximation algorithms for NP-hard problems
- Quantum algorithm design (Shor's, Grover's, VQE)
- Fine-grained complexity (SETH, ETH)
- Sublinear algorithms, streaming, sketching
- Online algorithms, competitive analysis

### Systems
- Distributed systems: consensus, replication, consistency models
- Operating systems: formally verified kernels (seL4, Fiasco.OC)
- Filesystems & storage: ZFS, Btrfs, io_uring, NVMe
- Networking: programmable data planes, eBPF, quantum networking
- Security: PQC migration, zero-trust, formal verification

### Programming Languages & Verification
- Type theory: dependent types, linear types, effect systems
- Verified compilers: CompCert, CakeML, verified LLVM passes
- Proof assistants: Coq, Lean 4, Isabelle
- Memory safety: Rust's borrow checker, formal verification of unsafe
- Quantum programming languages: Q#, Quipper

### Artificial Intelligence
- Foundation models: scaling laws, emergent capabilities, alignment
- Agentic AI: multi-agent coordination, tool use, memory
- Reinforcement learning: offline RL, RLHF, DPO
- AI safety: interpretability, corrigibility, constitutional methods
- Neuromorphic computing: spiking neural networks, Loihi

### Quantum Computing
- Quantum algorithms: Shor's, VQE, QAOA, quantum walks
- Quantum error correction: surface codes, topological codes
- Quantum networking: entanglement distribution, quantum repeaters
- Hybrid quantum-classical: variational algorithms, QAOA
- Quantum verification: testing, simulation, approximate verification

---

## ᛞ Dissertation Requirements
- Original, publishable research advancing the state of the art
- Minimum 2 papers at top-tier venues before defense
- Written dissertation (100-200 pages)
- Oral defense before committee of 3+ faculty + external examiner
