# Doctor of Philosophy in Virtual Reality — University of Yggdrasil, 2040

## ᚠ Overview: Building the Real Inside the Virtual

Virtual Reality in 2040 is no longer a novelty — it is a medium for work, therapy, education, art, and social connection. This PhD program explores VR as both technology and phenomenon: the engineering of immersive worlds AND the psychological, physiological, and philosophical implications of simulated experience.

## ᚱ Core Knowledge Domains

### VR Technology Stack (2040)
- **Head-mounted displays**: 8K+ per eye, 120°+ FOV, varifocal lenses, eye tracking
- **Haptic feedback**: force gloves, vibrotactile suits, ultrasound mid-air haptics
- **Tracking**: inside-out 6DoF, hand/finger tracking, full-body capture
- **Audio**: HRTF spatial audio, ambisonics, real-time room acoustic simulation
- **Rendering**: foveated rendering, neural radiance fields (NeRF), path tracing
- **Networking**: predictive rollback netcode, state synchronization for 1000+ users

### The Psychology of Presence
- **Slater's Place Illusion** — "I am in this place"
- **Slater's Plausibility Illusion** — "This is really happening"
- **Body ownership** — rubber hand illusion → virtual body illusion
- **Self-presence** — the sense of being inside one's virtual body
- **Social presence** — the illusion of being with real people

### Avatar Embodiment
- **Proteus Effect** — people behave consistently with their avatar's appearance
- **Full-body ownership** — visuo-motor synchrony → body transfer illusion
- **Identity flexibility** — using VR to explore gender, race, ability, species
- **Dissociative risks** — depersonalization, derealization from prolonged embodiment
- **Therapeutic embodiment** — using avatar switching for self-compassion, perspective-taking

### VR Therapy Applications
- **Exposure therapy**: phobias (heights, spiders, social), PTSD reconstructions
- **Pain management**: immersive distraction (SnowWorld for burn patients)
- **Rehabilitation**: stroke recovery, motor training, balance disorders
- **Social anxiety**: practice public speaking, job interviews, social situations
- **Body image**: eating disorder treatment through avatar embodiment
- **Psychedelic-assisted VR**: combined VR + psilocybin for end-of-life anxiety

## ᛏ 2040-Era Research Frontiers

- **Brain-computer VR interfaces** — direct neural input, bypassing hands entirely
- **Neural rendering** — AI generates photorealistic worlds from brain-activity-inferred imagination
- **Persistent virtual worlds** — VR spaces that exist 24/7 with economies and governance
- **VR for AI training** — simulated environments for embodied AI agents
- **Dream-like VR** — non-Euclidean spaces, time manipulation, impossible geometries
- **Cross-modal immersion** — smell, taste, temperature added to VR
- **Therapeutic game worlds** — clinically designed VR games that treat specific conditions
